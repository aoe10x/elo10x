ELO Calculation Methodology

Parameters & Calibration

Ratings are calculated sequentially in chronological order, using the following calibration rules:

  • Starting Rating: New players begin at 1000 ELO.
  • Dynamic K-Factor: Established players use a stable K = 32. For the first 21 matches (placement phase), the K-factor decays linearly from 100 to 32:
    K = 100 - n × (100 - 32) / 21
    This lets ratings converge quickly to a player's true skill level, modeled after the AoE2 Ranked placement formula.
  • Leaderboard Threshold: Players are hidden from the leaderboard until they have completed 15 matches (this choice was arbitrary).

ELO Update Formula

We compute individual rating adjustments by comparing a player's rating against the average rating of the opposing team:

Ep = 1 / (1 + 10(Ropp - Rp) / 400)
ΔRp = K × (S - Ep)
  • Rp: Player's ELO before the match.
  • Ropp: Average ELO of the opposing team.
  • Ep: Expected win probability (0 to 1).
  • S: Outcome (1 for Win, 0 for Loss).
  • ΔRp: Rating change (rounded to nearest integer).

For an interactive tool to simulate these ELO changes, see T-West's AoE2 ELO Calculator.

Match Eligibility & Gaps

To ensure competitive integrity, we only process matches that meet the following strict validation checks:

  • Strict 4v4 format: The match must contain exactly 8 tracked players.
  • Two distinct teams: Exactly 4 players per team.
  • Outcome verification: Exactly 4 winners and 4 losers must be reported. Ambiguous, draw, or bugged lobby reports are discarded.

Data Collection Note: Collection is manual and gaps exist. Matches are not recorded if "Record Game" was left unchecked in the lobby settings. Furthermore, public APIs do not support querying all custom lobbies globally; we must crawl by tracing individual player histories. That said, we strive to include as many games as possible.
